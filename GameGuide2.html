Introduction

Dota 2 is a multiplayer online battle arena video game and a sequel to Dota. Players control a hero that is a part of a team and work together to defeat the opposing team by destroying their ancient. The game includes heroes (playable characters), buildings (towers, barracks, ancients etc.) and creeps.

Game Basics

Dota 2 is played in matches between two teams of five players, with each team occupying and defending their own separate base on the map. Each of the ten players independently controls a powerful character, known as a "hero", who all have unique abilities and differing styles of play. During a match, players collect experience points and items for their heroes to successfully defeat the opposing team's heroes in player versus player combat. A team wins by being the first to destroy the other team's "Ancient", a large structure located within their base.

Heroes

Heroes (controlled by players) possess three attributes, one of which is their primary attribute. The attributes are:
Strength: Heroes with this primary attribute are warriors and can withstand more damage
Agility: These heroes have high attack speed and armor, and use regular attacks along with equipped items.
Intelligence: These heroes are masters of spells and abilities. They tend to have larger mana pools and much greater mana regeneration than other heroes
